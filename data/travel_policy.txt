1. GENERAL: All business travel requires prior written approval from your reporting manager.
2. EXPENSE SUBMISSION: Submit all travel claims via the company expense portal within 7 days of the trip.
3. REIMBURSEMENT PROCESSING: All valid claims will be processed within 15 days of submission.
4. HOTEL BOOKING: All hotel reservations should be made through the corporate travel portal.
5. ACCOMMODATION LIMITS: Employees must book standard, mid-range business hotels within city caps.
6. INCIDENTALS: Personal charges like in-room movies, mini-bar, or laundry are not reimbursable.
7. MEAL ALLOWANCE: Employees are provided a maximum daily per diem based on the destination.
8. MEAL CLAIMS: Meals must be documented; the per diem allowance cannot be rolled over to the next day.
9. ALCOHOL: Alcoholic beverages are considered non-reimbursable personal expenses.
10. TAXI CLAIMS: Use the most cost-effective ground transport; shared ride-sharing services are encouraged.
11. AIRPORT TRANSFERS: Taxi claims are permitted for travel to and from the airport and client offices.
12. TAXI RECEIPTS: Original or digital receipts are mandatory for all taxi claims exceeding the local limit.
13. NON-REIMBURSABLE: Fines for parking, traffic violations, or missed flights caused by personal negligence.
14. ADVANCES: Employees may request a travel advance for anticipated out-of-pocket expenses.
15. CURRENCY: Foreign travel expenses must be claimed in the local currency or converted using bank receipts.
16. COMPANIONS: The company will not reimburse any travel or meal expenses for non-employee companions.
17. ENTERTAINMENT: Pre-approved client entertainment meals require the names of all attendees on the receipt.
18. LOST RECEIPTS: Expenses over $\$10$ missing a proper receipt will not be reimbursed without a formal justification.
19. SAFETY: In case of emergencies, employees must immediately contact HR and their line manager.
20. POLICY AGREEMENT: By submitting a claim, employees confirm all expenses comply with this policy.
